About

I am an Assistant Professor in the School of Computing at the University of Georgia. Previously, I was a postdoctoral researcher at Stanford University, mentored by Prof. Jane Doe. I completed my Ph.D. at MIT, advised by Prof. Alan Turing.
My research focuses on artificial intelligence, machine learning, and their applications to real-world problems. I am particularly interested in developing robust and interpretable AI systems that can operate reliably in dynamic environments.

Research Interests

Machine Learning: Deep learning, reinforcement learning, transfer learning
Artificial Intelligence: Computer vision, natural language processing, robotics
Systems: Distributed machine learning, edge computing, AI safety
